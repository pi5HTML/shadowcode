Navigation

In v3.3, the top bar was replaced with a hamburger menu (the ≡ icon in the top-left corner). Click it to open the sidebar, which slides out over the page.

  • ShadowCode logo at the top of the sidebar — click it to go back to the home screen at any time.
  • Ciphers ▾ — a collapsible dropdown containing all five individual cipher tools. Click to expand or collapse.
  • Settings and Help are pinned at the bottom of the sidebar.
  • Clicking anywhere outside the sidebar (the dark overlay) closes it.

Where to start

Not sure which tool to use? Here's a quick guide:

  • Got a mystery cipher and don't know what it is? → Auto-Analysis
  • Want to send a hidden message to a friend? → Shift Cipher or Vigenère Cipher
  • Want to hide a message inside another piece of text? → Index Substitution Cipher
  • Want maximum difficulty to crack? → Dual Encrypt (e.g. Shift + Rail Fence)

Auto-Analysis

The fastest way to crack an unknown cipher. Paste your ciphertext in and hit Run Auto-Analysis. ShadowCode will try every cipher and key combination, score each result against a built-in English word base, and show the most likely decryption at the top.

It can also detect double encryption — enable that option in the Auto-Analysis panel if you suspect two ciphers were applied. Results are sorted by confidence percentage.

Example: paste in Khoor zruog and Auto-Analysis will find it's a Shift 3 in seconds.

Shift Cipher (Caesar Cipher)

Each letter is shifted a fixed number of places in the alphabet. To encrypt: type your message and pick a shift number (1–25), then click Encrypt. To decrypt manually: enter the shift value and click Manual Decrypt. If you don't know the shift, click Brute Force to try all 25 possibilities at once.

Example: "hello" with shift 3 → "khoor"

Index Substitution Cipher

Hides a message by replacing each letter with its position in a shared source text. Both the sender and receiver need the same source text to communicate.

To encrypt: paste a source text, type your message, click Encrypt. To decrypt: paste the same source text and the number key, click Decrypt.

Example source: "the quick brown fox" — the letter 't' is at index 1, 'h' at 2, 'e' at 3, and so on.

Vigenère Cipher

A stronger version of the Shift Cipher that uses a keyword instead of a single number. Each letter of your message is shifted by the corresponding letter of the keyword, cycling through the keyword as needed.

Enter your message and keyword, then click Encrypt or Decrypt. A keyword strength bar shows how secure your keyword is — longer keywords with varied letters are harder to crack.

Example: "hello" with keyword "LEMON" → "SIXFO"

Atbash Cipher

A simple mirror cipher — every letter is swapped with its opposite in the alphabet (A↔Z, B↔Y, C↔X, etc.). Encryption and decryption are the same operation, so just paste text and click Process.

Example: "hello" → "svool". Run it again on "svool" to get "hello" back.

Rail Fence Cipher

Scrambles your message by writing it in a zigzag pattern across a set number of "rails", then reading each rail left to right. To encrypt or decrypt: enter your text and the number of rails, then click the relevant button. Use the same rail count to reverse it.

Example: "helloworld" on 3 rails → "horel lwldo" (rails read row by row).

ShadowGlyph ShadowCode original

A ShadowCode-original cipher using a fixed substitution table with three rules: letters become symbols, digits become letters, and symbols become digits. Use Encode to encrypt plaintext, and Decode to reverse it. The two operations are distinct — Encode and Decode are not interchangeable.

ShadowGlyph is also tested by Auto-Analysis on the first pass, so if you paste in a ShadowGlyph-encoded message it will be detected and decoded automatically.

Example: "hello" → "(^=={" | Decode "(^=={" → "hello"

Note: ShadowGlyph is not available in Dual Encrypt.

Dual Encrypt

Applies two ciphers to your message back-to-back, making it significantly harder to crack. Choose any two ciphers from the dropdowns, fill in any required keys, and click Encrypt. To decrypt, reverse the process — apply the second cipher's decrypt first, then the first.

Tip: Shift 25 + Rail Fence 6–8 is extremely difficult to crack manually.

History Log

A running log of every encrypt, decrypt, and brute force operation since you opened ShadowCode. Click any entry to load it back into the relevant cipher tool. Nothing is saved or sent anywhere — the log clears when you close the tab (unless you enable persistent history in Settings).

Errors — what they mean and what to do

Cipher text / Plain text cannot be empty.

You clicked a button without typing anything in the input field. Enter your message first, then try again.

Decryption / Encryption shift value must be a number between 1 and 25.

The shift number field is blank, zero, or out of range. Enter a whole number from 1 to 25 in the shift field.

Warning: No strong English matches found.

Brute force ran but none of the 25 results looked like real English. The message may not be a Shift cipher, or it may be very short. Try Auto-Analysis instead, or check the input is correct.

Source Text and Index Key cannot be empty. / Source Text and Code Text cannot be empty.

Both the source text field and the key or message field must be filled in for Index Substitution to work. Fill in both fields and try again.

Source Text must contain letters after cleaning.

The source text you entered has no usable letters — it may be all numbers or symbols. Use a real sentence as your source text (e.g. "the quick brown fox").

Index Key must contain numbers separated by commas.

The index key field doesn't look like a valid key. It should be numbers separated by commas and spaces, like 1,6,20 5,21,3. Make sure you're pasting a key that was generated by ShadowCode.

DECRYPTION FAILED: One or more index numbers were out of bounds.

A number in the key is higher than the length of the source text. Make sure you're using the exact same source text that was used to encrypt the message — even one extra or missing word will cause this.

Message input cannot be empty. / Keyword cannot be empty.

Both the message field and the keyword field must be filled in for Vigenère to work. Fill in both and try again.

Keyword must contain only letters (A-Z).

Your Vigenère keyword has numbers, spaces, or symbols in it. Keywords must be letters only — for example LEMON or SECRET.

Input text cannot be empty. (Atbash)

The Atbash input field is empty. Type or paste the text you want to process and click the button again.

Input text cannot be empty. (ShadowGlyph)

The ShadowGlyph input field is empty. Type or paste your message and click Encode or Decode.

Number of rails must be between 2 and 10.

The rails field is empty, less than 2, or greater than 10. Enter a whole number from 2 to 10 and try again.

Plain text cannot be empty. (Rail Fence)

The Rail Fence input field is empty and you clicked Encrypt. Enter some text to encrypt and try again.

Cipher text cannot be empty. (Rail Fence)

The Rail Fence input field is empty and you clicked Decrypt or Brute Force. Paste the ciphertext you want to process and try again.

Cipher text cannot be empty for analysis. (Auto-Analysis)

You clicked Run Auto-Analysis without any text in the input field. Paste the ciphertext you want to analyse and try again.

You checked 'Includes Key' but left the key field empty.

You ticked the key option in Auto-Analysis but didn't fill in the key. Either enter a key or uncheck the option.

Error: Please enter a message to encrypt. (Dual Encrypt)

The plaintext field in Dual Encrypt is empty. Type a message before clicking Encrypt.

Cannot use the same cipher twice.

Both dropdowns in Dual Encrypt are set to the same cipher. Choose two different ciphers from the two dropdowns.

Vigenère and Index Substitution cannot be combined.

This specific combination is not supported. Pick a different second cipher — both work fine individually and with other ciphers.

First / Second cipher requires a key.

The key field for one of the two ciphers in Dual Encrypt is empty. Fill in both key fields (Atbash is the only cipher that doesn't need one).

Shift value must be a number between 1 and 25. (Dual Encrypt)

You selected Shift as one of the ciphers in Dual Encrypt but the shift key is not a valid number. Enter a number from 1 to 25.

⚠️ Auto-Analysis compatibility warning

This is a warning, not a hard error. It means fewer than 30% of words in your input were recognised by ShadowCode's word base, so encrypted results may score low in Auto-Analysis. The output is still likely correct. You can add custom words in the word base field to improve accuracy.

Detailed Workflow:

  • 1. The Source Text is cleaned: punctuation and spaces are removed (e.g., "testingwhyandwillitwork").
  • 2. Each number in the Index Key refers to a 1-based index of the cleaned Source Text.
  • 3. Any spaces in the Code/Key are preserved to define the word breaks in the final message.

Code Description:

The Atbash cipher is a simple mono-alphabetic substitution cipher where the alphabet is reversed. This means the first letter (A) maps to the last (Z), the second (B) maps to the second-to-last (Y), and so on. Because the process is reversible, the same action is used for both encoding and decoding.

Code Description:

The Rail Fence Cipher is a transposition cipher — instead of replacing letters, it rearranges them. Your message is written in a zigzag pattern across a set number of "rails" (rows), then the rows are read left to right one at a time to produce the ciphertext. For example, the message HELLO WORLD on 3 rails zigzags like a fence, scrambling the order. The key is simply how many rails were used. Unlike substitution ciphers, every letter in the ciphertext is also in the plaintext — just in a different position.

ShadowGlyph is a ShadowCode-original substitution cipher. On encode, every letter becomes a symbol, every digit becomes a letter, and every symbol becomes a digit — all using a fixed pre-coded table. Decode reverses this exactly. Characters not in the table pass through unchanged.
